Welcome to WeRoCon

Wearable Robotics
and Control Lab

We develop control strategies for powered lower-limb wearable robots—including prosthetic legs and exoskeletons—to restore mobility for people with reduced locomotion.

Our research brings together robotics, control theory, biomechanics and rehabilitation engineering. We study how humans move, how wearable machines interact with the body, and how intelligent control can make assistance safer, more comfortable and more useful in everyday environments.

At WeRoCon, we work across the complete research cycle: mathematical modelling, controller development, mechanical and embedded-system integration, simulation, experimental validation and human-subject studies. This multidisciplinary approach connects fundamental engineering questions with practical mobility challenges.

Our long-term goal is to develop cost-effective wearable robots that adapt to individual users and diverse walking conditions. The lab provides a collaborative environment for students and researchers interested in robotics, control systems, biomechanics, rehabilitation and human–robot interaction.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Saurav Kumar

Assistant Professor, Electrical Engineering, IIT Jodhpur

Dr. Kumar works on powered lower-limb wearable robots, adaptive control, legged-locomotion modelling and biomechanics. He received his PhD from the University of Texas at Dallas and completed postdoctoral research at Carnegie Mellon University.
