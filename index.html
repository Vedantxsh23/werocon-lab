Wearable Robotics
and Control Lab

The Wearable Robotics & Control (WeRoCon) Laboratory at IIT Jodhpur is dedicated to advancing the field of wearable robotic technologies for human mobility assistance and rehabilitation. The laboratory focuses on the research, design, development, and experimental validation of intelligent lower-limb prostheses, powered exoskeletons, and other assistive robotic systems aimed at improving the quality of life of individuals with mobility impairments.

WeRoCon Lab integrates robotics, control systems, biomechanics, artificial intelligence, embedded systems, and human-centered design to develop next-generation rehabilitation technologies. A major research emphasis is placed on creating intelligent and robust control strategies that enable wearable robots to adapt seamlessly to users and real-world environments.

The laboratory works in close collaboration with leading clinical partners, including AIIMS Jodhpur, facilitating translational research and extensive clinical evaluation. Researchers actively engage with individuals experiencing lower-limb amputation, spinal cord injury, stroke, multiple sclerosis, and other neuromuscular or orthopedic conditions to develop practical, user-centric solutions.

Through interdisciplinary research and real-world deployment, WeRoCon Lab aims to bridge the gap between cutting-edge robotic innovation and clinical rehabilitation, contributing to safer, smarter, and more accessible assistive technologies for restoring mobility and enhancing human independence.
